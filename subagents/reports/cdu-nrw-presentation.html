Key Findings 02 / 03

Three Areas Driving the Most Risk

Critical and high-severity issues grouped by impact domain

♿

Accessibility

2 critical · 3 high
Images have no alt text Hero slides, news thumbnails, and article images are invisible to screen readers sitewide.
Contact form is inaccessible Image CAPTCHA with no audio alternative completely blocks blind users from reaching the party.
No ARIA on interactive components Dropdowns, carousel, and social wall lack roles, aria-expanded, and live regions.
Hero carousel not keyboard-operable 40+ auto-rotating slides with no pause control — violates WCAG 2.2.2.
Heading structure is broken H1 reads "Startseite". H2 is used for "Slideshow" and "Suchformular" — not content.
Contact form — consent checkbox before fields, no error handling
🧭

User Experience

2 critical · 5 high
Cookie banner blocks all content On mobile the banner covers the entire viewport. First impression is a wall of legal text.
40-slide carousel as primary hero Content beyond slide 1 gets <1% engagement. 40 slides means virtually everything is invisible.
Mobile navigation hidden The hamburger menu is detached from the logo — easy to miss on first visit.
No news filtering or search 9+ pages of articles with pagination only — no way to browse by topic or date.
Fixed social media sidebar Outdated pattern from 2014, obscures content on smaller screens.
Mobile — cookie banner + carousel dominate; nav hard to find
⚡

Performance & Visual

2 high · 3 medium
50+ CSS files per page Severe render-blocking — every page loads 50+ separate stylesheets before rendering anything.
Drupal 7 is end-of-life EOL since November 2023 — no security updates, legacy jQuery, no Core Web Vitals support.
Fragmented header design Split dark/white header, awkward orange tagline strip, and 3 competing visual zones.
3 icon libraries loaded together FontAwesome, Material Icons, and Glyphicons coexist — visual inconsistency and wasted bytes.
Privacy policy dated 2018 Still references Privacy Shield (invalid since 2020) — active legal compliance risk.
Homepage — split header, social sidebar, dated visual language
Recommendations 03 / 03

Prioritised Action Plan

Grouped by effort and urgency — quick wins first, structural changes planned

Fix Now 0 – 4 weeks

Critical & Compliance Fixes

1

Add descriptive alt text to all images sitewide Use Drupal's Media module alt field — one change propagates everywhere.

2

Replace image CAPTCHA with reCAPTCHA v3 or a honeypot field Immediately re-opens the contact form to blind and low-vision users.

3

Update the privacy policy — remove Privacy Shield, add SCCs Active legal risk. Requires a lawyer. Publish with a "last updated" date.

4

Enable Drupal's CSS & JS aggregation Collapses 50+ files into 2–3. Zero code changes, massive performance gain.

5

Redesign cookie banner as a compact bottom bar Use an accessible CMP library (e.g. Klaro). Equal weight for accept/reject.

Expected impact: Resolves all 4 critical issues, closes the open GDPR risk, and delivers the biggest performance gain for the least effort.

Fix Soon 1 – 3 months

UX & Accessibility Improvements

1

Replace the 40-slide carousel with a 3-item featured grid Static cards with image, title, date and CTA. No auto-rotation.

2

Add ARIA attributes to all interactive components aria-expanded on menus, aria-current on active nav, landmarks on page regions.

3

Add news category tags and filter UI Drupal Views with exposed filters — no custom code. Dramatically improves discoverability.

4

Fix mobile navigation — make hamburger visible in header Attach toggle to the logo row. Label it "Menü" with icon + text.

5

Remove the fixed social media sidebar Move to footer. Add proper aria-labels to all social links.

Expected impact: Eliminates the most user-facing friction points. Likely increases content engagement and contact form completions significantly.

Plan & Invest 3 – 12 months

Strategic & Structural Overhaul

1

Migrate from Drupal 7 to Drupal 10 or headless CMS EOL platform is a security and performance liability. Plan a 3–6 month migration.

2

Full visual redesign with a unified design system One icon library, 8px spacing grid, consistent typography scale, modern header.

3

Flatten navigation to 2 levels with a mega-menu for Partei Removes 3-level deep nesting. Dramatically improves mobile usability.

4

Replace social media embeds with server-side cached feeds No more blank "aktivieren" boxes. Better performance, GDPR-safe by default.

5

Commission a full WCAG 2.1 AA audit post-redesign Verify contrast ratios, keyboard flows, and screen reader compatibility end-to-end.

Expected impact: Transforms the site from a legacy Drupal 7 installation into a modern, accessible, performant platform that reflects the party's standing.
